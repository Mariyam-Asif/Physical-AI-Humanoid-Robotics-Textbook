# Welcome to the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook

This textbook provides a comprehensive guide to the exciting and rapidly evolving fields of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. Designed for industry practitioners, engineering students, and robotics hobbyists, this resource will equip you with the foundational knowledge and practical skills needed to understand, design, and implement intelligent robotic systems.

## What You Will Learn

Across 13 weeks, organized into 4 core modules, you will explore topics ranging from the robotic nervous system (ROS 2) and digital twins (Gazebo, Unity) to the AI robot brain (NVIDIA Isaac) and advanced vision-language-action integration.

<Callout type="learning-objectives">
**Key Learning Objectives of this Introduction:**
*   Grasp the interdisciplinary nature of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.
*   Familiarize yourself with the textbook's modular structure and learning path.
*   Understand the prerequisites and how to get the most out of this course.
</Callout>

## Textbook Structure

This textbook is structured to facilitate a clear and progressive learning journey:

*   **Modules**: The content is divided into four main modules, each covering a major thematic area of physical AI and humanoid robotics.
*   **Weeks**: Each module is further broken down into weekly lessons, providing a manageable pace for learning.
*   **Chapters**: Within each week, individual chapters delve into specific topics, offering detailed explanations, code examples, and hands-on exercises.

## Getting Started

To begin your journey, we recommend:

1.  **Explore the Modules**: Navigate through the sidebar to explore the different modules and their weekly breakdowns.
2.  **Review Prerequisites**: Each module and chapter will clearly state any necessary prerequisites.
3.  **Engage with Exercises**: Apply your knowledge by working through the hands-on exercises integrated into the chapters.
4.  **Consult the Glossary**: Refer to the [Glossary](/docs/glossary) for definitions of key terms.
5.  **Check References**: Utilize the [References](/docs/references) section for further reading and source attribution.

We are excited for you to embark on this learning adventure!
